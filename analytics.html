02 — Key Insights

What the Data Is Telling You

Six findings that cut across all channels and directly inform the action plan.

When visitors reach high-intent pages, they convert wellThe /request-a-demo/ page converts at 23% — that's strong. The problem is discovery: only 3,865 people found it in 90 days across ~182,000 sessions. The bottleneck is architecture, not the page itself.
Referral is the most efficient channel — but needs validation3.12% session key event rate, 26% of all demo conversions. A portion of this traffic includes HubSpot-tracked links from existing customers and partner onboarding. The likely new-prospect drivers are partner ecosystems: Snowflake, Databricks, G2, and Gartner Peer Insights. If confirmed, this is the highest-leverage distribution play available.
Organic Search is the volume engine — not the conversion engine49% of all sessions, 0.57% key event rate. Blog readers are arriving on educational content (data pipelines, RAG, LLMs) and have no consistent path to a demo. The content is excellent. The conversion pathways are not.
Display needs an audit before any budget decision16,503 sessions, 0.23% key event rate, 2 demo conversions. GA4 last-touch attribution doesn't capture view-through conversions — which matter in a 30–90 day enterprise sales cycle. Broad prospecting on GDN: reallocate. Account-based retargeting against an ICP list: keep and add view-through tracking. Don't cut without knowing which one you're running.
The chatbot (Berkeley/Qualified) is not a conversion driver104 email captures from 31 unique users over 90 days. Only 2 conversations started. The "41 meetings booked in chat" metric requires CRM validation — likely mis-attributed conversions that would have happened anyway. A simple inline blog form (Talk To Us) generated 21 conversions with no chatbot. Direct demo requests generated 122 conversions without it. Recommendation: Remove the chatbot. Competitors (Datadog, Hex, Torq) don't use them.
AI-referred traffic is small but punches significantly above its weight4,285 sessions (2.1% of total) from ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, Claude, and Copilot. Perplexity converts at 1.55% — equal to Direct traffic. Copilot at 3.08% — equal to Referral. These visitors arrive informed and intent-driven. The GEO opportunity is already working.

🤔
The "Request a Demo" Paradox

/request-a-demo/ has a 23% conversion rate — exceptional performance. The page works. But only 3,865 visitors reached it in 90 days (9.8% of homepage traffic).

Meanwhile, "Request a Demo" is the primary CTA in the navigation and homepage hero. So why isn't it getting more traffic?

Hypothesis: The CTA is too high-commitment for early-stage visitors. "Demo" implies a sales call. Visitors who aren't ready bounce instead of exploring. The 39,454 homepage visits → 263 conversions (0.67%) suggests most visitors leave without engaging.

💡 Suggested Fix
  • Elevate "Take a Tour" to co-primary CTA (not secondary) — Lower commitment, self-service exploration
  • Test softer homepage CTAs: "See How It Works" → Product tour, not demo form
  • Multi-path approach: Give visitors 3 options at different commitment levels (Tour / Pricing / Demo)
  • Promote O'Reilly Report prominently — 68% conversion, gated asset = middle ground between tour and demo

Recommended Actions

Immediate — No Site Changes Required
  • Pull referral source breakdown to confirm if Snowflake, Databricks, G2, Gartner drive 26% of demo conversions
  • Wire high_intent and form_start events to HubSpot SDR alerts (1,738 and ~650 fires/90d currently not triggering outreach)
  • Audit Display campaign targeting before reallocating budget
Short-term — Site & Content Changes
  • Promote O'Reilly Report prominently — 68% conversion on 1,402 views. Feature in homepage hero or primary nav.
  • Add blog-to-demo conversion paths — Inline CTAs, sticky demo bar, or exit-intent on high-traffic posts (40–76s engagement = qualified readers)
  • Elevate "Take a Tour" to primary CTA — Co-equal with "Request a Demo" in hero/nav (self-guided tour = closest to free trial in demo-gated model)
  • Add FAQ schema to AI-landing blog posts — Data pipeline, RAG, LLM posts already cited by ChatGPT/Perplexity. Structured markup improves surfacing.
  • Investigate Claude.ai gap — 202 users, solid engagement, only 1 key event. Pull landing pages for Claude sessions.

07 — Roadmap

Before Anything Else — Foundation

Three things that need to be in place before the rest of the roadmap will work. Without them, the signals exist but nothing acts on them.

Foundational Infrastructure

These unlock everything downstream. Low cost, high leverage.

GA4 → HubSpot Signal Wiring
Connect high_intent and form_start GA4 events to HubSpot workflows and SDR Slack alerts. 1,738 intent signals firing per quarter with zero downstream action. No site changes required.
Referral Source Validation
Pull source-level referral breakdown in GA4. Confirm whether Snowflake, Databricks, G2, or Gartner are driving the 26% of demo conversions. The entire partner investment decision depends on this.
Display Campaign Audit
Identify what the current display campaign is targeting before cutting or keeping it. Broad GDN prospecting: reallocate. Account-based retargeting (ICP list): add view-through tracking. This decision requires one conversation, not a budget cut.
