🤔 Why the Primary CTA Isn't Working

Why? "Demo" implies a sales call. Too high-commitment for early-stage visitors who are still learning what Monte Carlo does.

🎯 Diagnosis: Why the Hero Isn't Working

1. Too High-Commitment CTA

"Request a Demo" is the wrong primary CTA for early-stage visitors.

Demo = sales call. Visitors who land on the homepage for the first time aren't ready to commit to a 30-60 minute discovery call. They're still in the "What does this company do?" phase.

Evidence: Only 9.8% of homepage visitors reach the demo page. The other 90.2% bounce or explore elsewhere.

2. "Take a Tour" is Buried

"Take a Tour" is positioned as a secondary/fallback CTA.

But this is the closest thing to a free trial Monte Carlo offers. In a demo-gated model, the self-guided tour should be co-primary, not secondary.

Competitors (Datadog, Hex) lead with "Free Trial" — structural advantage. Monte Carlo's tour is the right instinct but needs equal prominence.

3. No Middle Ground

The homepage offers binary choices: high-commitment demo or low-commitment tour.

Missing: Medium-commitment option. Something like:

  • O'Reilly Report (68% conversion when surfaced!)
  • "See How It Works" → Product walkthrough video
  • "Get Pricing" (2,496 views, 2.5% conversion — clear buyer intent)

Visitors need a path between "just browsing" and "ready to talk to sales."
