Engagement Phases
Three ways to start.

Each phase builds on the last. Choose the level of discovery that fits where Monte Carlo is right now, then we use everything we learn to define the rebuild.

Phase 01
Diagnose: Website Audit & Next Steps

The foundational engagement. We go deep on what exists, what's working, and what's broken, then hand you a clear, prioritized roadmap.

$10K
Starting engagement
What We Do
  • Full inventory of all pages, forms, and content assets on the current site
  • UX heuristic evaluation: how the site performs against modern usability standards
  • Identification of conversion blockers preventing demo requests
  • Form audit: purpose, redundancy, and recommended consolidation
  • Navigation and IA review: how many clicks to a CTA?
  • Scroll depth analysis: where do visitors drop off?
  • Mobile experience audit
And Then
  • Brand and messaging check: does the site reflect the AI observability story?
  • Page speed and Core Web Vitals assessment
  • SEO health baseline: which pages drive traffic and must be preserved?
  • Quick wins list: things that can be improved in 30 to 60 days with no rebuild required
  • Prioritized issue list by severity, effort, and impact
  • Final audit report with recommended path forward
Deliverable

A comprehensive audit report and prioritized recommendation document. This is the brief that drives everything downstream.

Phase 02
Define: Diagnose + Competitive Intelligence + Market Research

Everything in Diagnose, plus a structured look at how Monte Carlo stacks up in the market and a research layer that ensures the rebuilt site speaks to the right buyers in the language they actually use.

$17K
Includes Diagnose
Competitive Layer
  • All deliverables from Diagnose
  • Competitor website audit: messaging, IA, CTA model, proof points, visual design, and positioning across top competitors
  • Side-by-side comparison across 8 key criteria: messaging clarity, CTA strength, demo friction, social proof, AI narrative, mobile experience, content depth, and visual design
  • Aspirational site benchmarks: 3 to 4 sites from adjacent categories that set the standard for where Monte Carlo's site should go
  • "Moments of differentiation" brief: the specific claims and narrative angles that should anchor the homepage
Market Research Layer
  • 3 primary buyer personas defined and mapped
  • Per-persona job-to-be-done: what problem are they solving when they land on the site?
  • Language and terminology audit: are you speaking the way your buyers actually search and talk?
  • Entry point analysis: where are buyers coming from and what do they expect to see?
  • Content gap analysis: what questions are buyers asking that the site doesn't answer?
  • GEO readiness check: is the site structured in a way that AI tools like Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity can surface and recommend it?
  • Demo request friction map: from first click to booked call, where does intent bleed out?
  • Messaging hierarchy: what goes above the fold, in the middle, at the bottom
Deliverable

Competitive analysis report, differentiation brief, buyer persona profiles, content gap report, and messaging hierarchy framework.

Phase 03
Design: Define + Customer Lifecycle Journey

The complete engagement. Everything in Define, plus a full map of the customer lifecycle so the rebuilt site works for the entire relationship, not just acquisition.

$23K
Includes Define & Diagnose
Lifecycle Layer
  • All deliverables from Define
  • End-to-end lifecycle map: Awareness, Consideration, Decision, Onboarding, Retention, Advocacy
  • Website touchpoint mapping: where the site touches, or should touch, each stage of the journey
  • Post-demo experience audit: does the site support or contradict the sales conversation?
  • Customer advocacy strategy: how to better leverage existing enterprise clients throughout the site experience
Advanced Strategy
  • Expansion and upsell content mapping: does the site support existing customers exploring additional capabilities?
  • Lifecycle-aware CTA strategy: different calls to action for net-new vs. in-evaluation vs. existing customers
  • Account-based experience recommendations: how the site can behave differently for known vs. unknown visitors
Deliverable

Full customer lifecycle map and journey-aware site strategy document.

What Happens After
