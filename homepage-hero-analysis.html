⚠️ The Problem

Homepage has highest traffic but lowest engagement. Visitors "orient and click through quickly" — it functions as a navigation hub, not a conversion driver. The 0.67% key event rate is the metric that matters, and it's poor.

🎯 Above-the-Fold CTAs (What the Data Says)

Primary CTA: "Request a Demo"

🔴 High-Commitment Barrier

The paradox: "Request a Demo" is the primary CTA on the homepage hero, yet only 3,865 people reached the demo page in 90 days (9.8% of homepage traffic).

When people DO reach the demo page, it converts at 23% — exceptional. But most homepage visitors never click through. Why? "Demo" implies a sales call. Too high-commitment for early-stage visitors.

Secondary CTA: "Take a Tour"

⚠️ Buried as Secondary

Current positioning: Secondary/fallback CTA. But this is the closest thing to a free trial Monte Carlo offers. In a demo-gated model, the self-guided tour should be co-primary, not secondary.

Competitors (Datadog, Hex) lead with "Free Trial" — structural advantage. Monte Carlo's tour is the right instinct but needs equal prominence.

🔍 What We Can Infer

✓ Homepage Traffic is Strong

39,454 views (21.7% of all site traffic) — homepage is being found. SEO, paid search, and referrals are working. The issue is not discovery of the homepage.

🔴 Engagement is Weak

13.6 seconds average time suggests visitors are scanning, not reading. They're orienting ("What does this company do?") and then clicking away — likely to product pages or blog. The hero is not compelling them to convert on the spot.

🔴 Conversion is Poor

0.67% key event rate (263 events from 39,454 views) is low. For context:

  • Demo page converts at 23% when reached
  • O'Reilly Report converts at 68% when reached
  • Blog readers engage for 40-76 seconds

The homepage is the weak link. When visitors get deeper into the site, they convert. The hero is failing to move them forward.

Hypothesis: Lower-commitment CTA will increase click-through rate. More visitors will engage with tour → qualify themselves → convert to demo at higher quality.

📋 Summary: Homepage Hero is Underperforming

The Diagnosis

Highest traffic, lowest engagement, poor conversion.

The hero is functioning as a navigation checkpoint — visitors scan the headline, realize what the company does, and then click to product pages or blog to learn more. They're not converting in the hero because the CTAs are too high-commitment ("Request a Demo") or too buried ("Take a Tour").

The Fix (Based on Available Data)

  • Elevate "Take a Tour" to co-primary CTA — Equal visual weight with "Request a Demo"
  • Test softer CTA copy: "See How It Works" → Tour (not "Request a Demo")
  • Add O'Reilly Report to hero: 68% conversion rate, gated asset = middle ground
  • Multi-path approach: Give visitors 3 options at different commitment levels (Tour / Resource / Demo)

⚠️ But First: Get the Granular Data

Without CTA-level click tracking and video engagement data, we're making informed guesses. Pull GA4 event data or set up custom tracking before making major hero changes. Test hypotheses with data, not assumptions.
